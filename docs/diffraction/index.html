Diffraction Limitation Calculator

A tool to help calculate the diffraction limit of a camera system.

This calculator helps you determine the diffraction limit of your camera system. That is, it estimates the smallest detail that can be resolved given your aperture and contrast threshold. A contrast threshold of 0% means there is no difference between light and dark areas, while a higher percentage means a more noticeable difference is visible. This means the absolute limit of resolution is at 0%, but in practical terms, higher contrast thresholds such as 25-50% are often more useful for real-world photography. Note that a wavelength of 520 nm (green light) is used by default, this is due to it being in the middle of the visible spectrum and a reasonable choice for general photography.
